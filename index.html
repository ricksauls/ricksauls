1999–2012
Enterprise Retail Foundation

Built inside the operational backbone of Walmart's retail ecosystem.

I began my career at Walmart while completing my studies in Computer Information Systems & Quantitative Analysis, initially joining as an intern supporting warehouse and distribution systems.

That early exposure to enterprise-scale logistics and retail infrastructure shaped how I think about systems, scale, and operational efficiency.

Following my internship, I transitioned into a full-time role where I worked on tools supporting warehouse operations, merchandising workflows, and financial planning systems used by category teams.

27 Distribution Centers 5× DC Network Growth $3.2B Category Management
Business Impact
In Distribution & Logistics, the work centered on building tools that drove efficiency and reduced cost — the core mandate of a cost-center business unit. Scale was also expanding fast: the distribution network saw more than 5× growth in seven years, and the systems my team built had to keep cost performance improving as the footprint multiplied.
In Merchandise Planning, the impact shifted to top-line and margin levers — driving sales, expanding profit margins, and optimizing inventory turns across large product categories. Each is a core driver of a successful retail business, and the work meant translating financial analysis into clear, actionable guidance for the buyer and merchant teams making the calls.
Key Initiatives
Warehouse Management Systems
  • As a software developer and later team lead, delivered solutions across Receiving, Replenishment, Order-Filling, Load Planning, Shipping, and Quality Assurance.
  • Integrated voice recognition, wireless touchscreens, and wireless scanners — leveraging hands-free and mobile technology as key drivers of operational efficiency.
Merchandise Planning
  • Annual budgeting of sales, profit, inventory turns, and other key metrics by category and subcategory at the week level.
  • Ongoing performance analysis against plan at weekly, monthly, quarterly, and annual levels — surfacing what was working, where to adjust, and translating insights into action.
  • Financial analysis supporting annual category resets, aligning assortment and layout changes with annual financial goals.
  • Promotional and feature planning support, covering planning, execution, monitoring, and performance reporting.
What It Built
This chapter established the operational and systems fluency that shaped everything that followed — a deep understanding of how inventory moves at scale, how cost centers are managed for efficiency, and how technology either accelerates or constrains large organizations. It's the foundation that made everything that came next faster, sharper, and grounded in how retail actually works.
2012–2015
Agency, Innovation & Data Strategy

Innovation and data strategy for the world's biggest retailers and CPGs.

After Walmart, I spent three years in the agency world across two firms. My first role focused on delivering innovation to Walmart across the organization, partnering with teams spanning Global Business Process, Human Resources Training & Development, and Merchandise Operations. My second role, at a different agency, centered on data management and business intelligence solutions for some of the world's largest CPGs — Coca-Cola and Johnson & Johnson among them.

Walmart — Innovating For The World's Largest Retailer & CPG's
Business Impact
At the first agency, the business impact mirrored my earlier work in Walmart Information Technology — driving operational efficiency through emerging technology. The mandate was familiar: identify where the right tools could remove friction and create lift across enterprise teams.
At the second agency, the impact shifted to visibility. The work centered on translating the massive volumes of Walmart data — thousands of stores, millions of items — into clear, actionable intelligence that gave CPG clients a clearer view of their business and a sharper basis for the decisions in front of them.
Key Initiatives
Innovation
  • Identified, sold, and delivered innovation initiatives at Walmart through close collaboration with internal agency partners and Walmart stakeholders.
  • Applied emerging mobile and web technologies to expand what was possible across enterprise workflows.
Data Management & Business Intelligence
  • Partnered with Tier 1 CPG clients and internal agency teams to scope and deliver tailored data and BI engagements.
  • Delivered end-to-end data work — ingestion, cleansing, integration, and presentation.
  • Built performance dashboards, reporting, insights, and analytics that translated raw data into decisions clients could act on.
What It Built
While both roles were rooted in technology and data, the work also introduced a new dimension for me: sales. I quickly learned to "always be selling," and the experience of pitching solutions, growing accounts, and owning client relationships expanded my perspective in ways that later shaped my approach to entrepreneurship.
2015–2023
Building & Scaling Companies

Built, scaled, and exited Amazon-focused eCommerce companies.

Over eight years, I co-founded and scaled two Amazon-focused eCommerce companies from the ground up. The first, OmniiX, was a services company and one of Amazon's first certified agency partners. We grew the business to acquisition within six years. The second, chargeguard, was a tech-first company focused on Amazon deductions management — helping vendors manage and recover the chargebacks and deductions that erode margins.

Across both companies, the work was entrepreneurship in its truest form — wearing every hat: setting vision, driving sales and marketing, running operations, building teams, owning client services, and adapting as Amazon evolved. The throughline was building operational systems, shaping marketplace strategy, and creating scalable infrastructure for digital commerce.

1 Service Agency 1 Tech-First Agency Amazon Focused
Business Impact
At OmniiX, our impact showed up directly in our clients' growth. By combining Amazon subject matter expertise, deep retail knowledge, and agency service experience, we consistently delivered sales growth that outpaced even Amazon's rapid platform expansion — in one case taking a client from $60K to $1.5M in annual revenue.
At chargeguard, the impact was a direct deposit to the bank. Through innovative technology and operational expertise, we recovered millions of dollars for clients — money that had otherwise been lost to deductions and chargebacks.
Key Initiatives
OmniiX
  • Built the business from the ground up: validated the market, sold the vision, stood up operations, and hired and trained the team to deliver.
  • Stayed ahead of changes on the Amazon platform and translated each shift into a competitive edge for our clients — winning consistently, not just keeping up.
chargeguard
  • Created an online training course that served two purposes: generating early revenue to fund the company's launch and developing a network of 200+ sales and marketing specialists.
  • Developed proprietary technology for Amazon vendor deductions management — first to capture, organize, and surface deductions data as the foundation for selling the solution, then to manage the dispute lifecycle and recover erroneous fees on behalf of clients.
What It Built
This chapter built something no role inside another company could. Founding, scaling, and exiting two businesses isn't just the sum of the work — it's the conviction to start, the resilience to operate under uncertainty, and the judgment to know when to push, when to adapt, and when to let go. It built a clear-eyed sense of what makes a business worth building, worth scaling, and worth buying — and the experience of doing it twice, the only way to know the first time wasn't luck.
